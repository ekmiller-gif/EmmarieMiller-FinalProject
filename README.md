## Additional Reflection Questions
 - What was your **overall approach** to tackling this project?

    My overall approach to this project was to handle the story first, as it is primarily narrative driven. Before even touching the main game loop, I constructed my architecture diagrams and game map, as well as a flowchart that documents all possible outcomes. This was really helpful and made the prgramming part a breeze since I didn't have to decide everything storywise while I was programming. It also gave me a place to reference all my commands and plotlines without having to scour through the code.

 - What **new thing(s)** did you learn / figure out in completing this project?

    The do/while loop feature was a cool discovery and was much easier than a continuous while loop. (more later)

 - Is there anything that you wish you had **implemented differently**?

    I kind of wish I had declared most of my objects (apple, windows, room) before entering the conditionals so that I didn't have to define a new one each time it came up, but by the time I figured this out it was a difficult mess to clean up, so I just kept using different types of apples, which was actually helpful to find my place.

 - If you had **unlimited time**, what additional features would you implement?

    If I had more time, I would've liked to have a more interactive map with more commands for movement and exploration.

 - What was the most helpful **piece of feedback** you received while working on your project? Who gave it to you?

    Jasmine put in the slack the link to the website I used to create my flowchart, and recommended using it if you need help visualizing your map/story. The flowchart ended up bein one of the most important pieces of reference for the program!

 - If you could go back in time and give your past self some **advice** about this project, what hints would you give?

    Give yourself time and start early - it seems like a dounting task but its not that hard when you break it into pieces and plan out your code.
